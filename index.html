Target Communities Services

Target Communities

- Older Adults: Many older adults live in conditions that make them vulnerable to extreme heat events. UHRI aims to assist these individuals by implementing effective cooling solutions and fostering social connections through communal green spaces.

- Low-Income Populations: Poor housing conditions and limited resources make low-income populations particularly susceptible to heat-related illnesses. UHRI will focus on making cooling solutions more accessible and affordable to these communities.

- People in Poor Health: Individuals with chronic conditions, disabilities, and mobility constraints are especially vulnerable to extreme heat. UHRI will tailor its services to ensure the unique needs of this population are addressed, for example, by offering in-home assessments and solutions for those with mobility constraints.

Services

- Cooling Solutions Implementation: Assist communities in the implementation of affordable and effective cooling solutions such as the installation of reflective 'cool' roofs, wall insulations, and the use of cool paving materials in community spaces.

- Green Urban Development: Guide and support the development of green spaces, urban gardens, and green roofs in densely built neighborhoods. Also, lead tree planting initiatives to provide natural shading and cooling.

- Education and Training: Develop and deliver online and in-person educational programs about UHI effects and simple, cost-effective mitigation strategies.

- Policy Advocacy: Advocate for policies that protect vulnerable communities from extreme heat, pushing for stricter building codes and advocating for subsidies or financing options to make cooling solutions more affordable for low-income households.
